Why ReceiptsMinder?

Consumer Rights for Data Portability
  • Consumer data is not only accessible to retailers but using RM it will be available to consumers as well.
  • RM provides a digital warranty and thereby improving the ability for the consumer in product warranty management.
  • Eliminating receipt loss - a study by Capital One shows that 42% of people did not reclaim their money due to lost receipts, which resulted in an average loss of £ 168 per worker per year of London workers working for Capital One (Guryakov, 2012).
  • RM can enhance the experience of claiming tax-free refunds, travel expenses, and processing warranties, thus making the process of claiming expenses more efficient and faster than scanning paper receipts.
Sustainability
  • Sustainability and protection of the environment are definitely key drivers to the adoption of digital receipts in the country such as the UK
  • Help to avoid cutting 200,000 trees annually to print 11.3 Billion receipts in the UK only.
  • Help to minimize the use of paper receipts, which usually contain bisphenol A (BPA) and bisphenol S (BPS) that is used to produce a long-term print through a fast process via heat induction at the point of sale. The absorption of bisphenol happens through the skin when touching the thermal paper receipts, and therefore affects cashier personnel as well as all consumers.
Mitigating Tax fraud
  • Taxes are the base of every government and therefore, a reliable tax collection process is needed. The digital transformation changes the interaction between tax authorities and taxpayers. Using RM may help in avoiding tax fraud.
  • RM technology infrastructure could well support the introduction of consumer-directed digital receipts.
Data Privacy
  • RM promises to keep private data securely on cloud and consumers expect transparent communication and intuitive consent options to give, review and withdraw their approval for processing their data.
  • For example, consumers might hesitate to insert their email address or mobile phone number at the point of sales. RM gives a unique identity number to each consumer, which can be used at a point of sales system. Email address or mobile phone will only be used for retrieving OPT for registration and login.
